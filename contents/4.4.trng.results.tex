\section{Results}
\label{sec:trng-results}

\subsection{NIST SP~800-90B Entropy Estimates}
Table~\ref{tab:nist90b} reports non-IID min-entropy estimates from
$1\mathrm{M}$ samples per source across the nine NIST SP~800-90B
estimators.

\paragraph{4M Entropy Sources.}

All three 4M conditions produce $\hat{H}_{\min} < 0.01$ bits/symbol
(0.0060, 0.0051, and 0.0079 for RT-A, RT-B, and 55\textdegree{}C,
respectively). The compression estimator returns the lowest values
(0.0004 and 0.0046 for RT-A/B), confirming that latency sequences are
highly compressible. All nine SP~800-90B estimators return near-zero
values for every 4M condition, ruling out any estimator-specific
artifact and confirming a fundamental collapse of source entropy under
the runtime measurement regime. The near-zero estimates are consistent
with a latency distribution that has collapsed to a near-deterministic,
narrow support. The compression estimator value of $4\times10^{-4}$
bits/symbol for RT-A approaches the theoretical minimum for a
length-$1\mathrm{M}$ sequence over a 256-bin alphabet. The
elevated-temperature condition yields a marginal improvement to
$\hat{H}_{\min} = 0.0079$ bits/symbol; while thermal activation can
increase filament stochasticity by reducing the energy barrier for
vacancy migration~\cite{yu2012switching}, this effect is insufficient to
rescue the 4M entropy source. The dominant limitation is structural, not
thermal. The runtime entropy collapse is attributed to structural
bursting effects. During characterization, the full sweep of burst
configurations explored the bursting space under a diversity of stimuli
(subsequent bursts are rotated by a single word independent of burst
size). Under the runtime sequence, fixed-width bursts at burst-aligned
offsets within the reserved 256\,B region collapse nearly all write
latency variance. This sensitivity to stimulus disqualifies the 4M as a
viable TRNG platform and exposes a fundamental limitation of
characterization-only entropy evaluations.

\paragraph{8M Entropy Sources.}
The 8M device produces $\hat{H}_{\min}$ of 2.4973 (RT-A), 1.6500 (RT-B),
and 1.9844 (55\textdegree{}C) bits/symbol. The $t$-Tuple estimator is
binding across all three conditions (2.8370, 1.7358, 2.2754), consistent
with short-range thermal coupling between adjacent writes. The 0.34-bit
restart penalty on RT-A reflects modest startup bias as the filament
distribution converges after reset. Device-to-device variation of 0.85
bits/symbol between RT-A and RT-B underscores the necessity of per-chip
characterization. The 55\textdegree{}C result suggests thermal
activation provides a real but bounded boost to filament stochasticity.
The contrast with the 4M device is attributed to burst complexity. The
8M crossbar is driven with 32-word bursts while the 4M uses 4-word
bursts. The longer burst aggregates more switching events per
transaction, increasing thermal heterogeneity across bitcells and
reducing the degree to which any individual filament's history can
dominate the aggregate latency measurement. This suggests a minimum
burst complexity threshold below which the single-stimulus feedback loop
is not broken by accumulated stochastic contributions from other cells.

\subsection{NIST SP~800-22 and PractRand}
Given the near-zero entropy of the 4M under the runtime sequence, it is
excluded from TRNG statistical validation. Table~\ref{tab:nist22}
reports SP~800-22 results for 256\,KB of SHA-256-conditioned output from
all three 8M sources across two independent runs each. All 15 tests pass
for every source-run combination. $p$-values are well-distributed across
$(0,1)$; the lowest observed is 0.01 (Binary Matrix Rank,
55\textdegree{}C-R1). All three streams pass PractRand through 256\,KB
with no anomalies at any evaluation threshold, confirming that no
artifacts from the repeating $S_{opt}$ sequence or wear-leveling pattern
persist through conditioning. The final TRNG achieves a throughput of
1.7\,kbits/s.

\subsection{Relationship to Prior Work}
The 4M failure does not disqualify COTS ReRAM entropy harvesting in
general, nor does it imply that the device contains no stochastic
dynamics. Arul et al.~\cite{tolga2024trng} obtained non-trivial
min-entropy from the same device family by applying randomized write
patterns followed by aggressive von Neumann
debiasing~\cite{von1963various}. The structural latency component
removed in Section~\ref{sec:trng-structural} was on the order of tenths
of a millisecond, while the timer resolution used
in~\cite{tolga2024trng} likely operated at the MHz level. The present
work confirms that under the more conservative fixed-sequence regime
demanded by a stationary entropy source evaluation, the 4M device cannot
sustain an exploitable entropy rate. ECC knowledge is necessary but not
sufficient for an efficient COTS ReRAM TRNG; the underlying crossbar
architecture must not dominate timing behavior at the chosen burst
configuration, and runtime evaluation under the intended operational
sequence is required.
